Ivory Coast cocoa farmers hope for more sunny days

ABIDJAN, Nov 4 (Reuters) - Heavy rainfall last week in most of Ivory Coast’s cocoa-growing regions was accompanied by some of the sunniest days in months, improving bean quality, farmers said on Monday. Ivory Coast, the world’s top cocoa producer, is on the cusp of the dry season which runs from mid-November to March. Farmers said more rainfall mixed with sunny spells was needed before the arrival of December’s dry harmattan winds for next year’s harvest to develop well. But continuing heavy rains could cause cherelles and small pods to fall from trees and reduce the size of next year’s crop, they added. While consistent sunshine throughout last week helped farmers dry beans properly, some reported the high moisture content had caused damage to trees.
